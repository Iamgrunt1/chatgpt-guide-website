            <h2 class="section-title">3. Essential ChatGPT Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts</h2>

            <div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-8">

                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 1: Be Specific and Clear.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">The more precise your prompt, the better the output. Avoid vague requests.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Write a 200-word blog post about the benefits of meditation for stress reduction, targeting beginners."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 1: Be Vague or General.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Avoid giving broad, undefined instructions, as this limits the quality of the response.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Tell me about meditation."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 2: Define the Role.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Tell ChatGPT what persona to adopt (e.g., "Act as a marketing expert").</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Act as a seasoned travel agent. Suggest a 7-day itinerary for a family trip to Rome."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 2: Neglect Setting a Role.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Don't expect a specific tone or perspective without guiding it.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Give me a travel itinerary for Rome." (Without specifying who it's for or the tone)</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 3: Set Output Constraints.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Specify length, format (e.g., "in 3 bullet points, no more than 50 words").</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Summarize this article in 3 bullet points, no more than 50 words."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 3: Leave Output Format Undefined.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Without clear format instructions, responses might be unstructured or too verbose.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Summarize this article."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 4: Provide Context.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Give it relevant background information for a more accurate response.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "I'm planning a camping trip in a forest with bears. What precautions should I take?"</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 4: Withhold Necessary Context.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Lack of context can lead to irrelevant or incomplete answers.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "What precautions should I take for a trip?"</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 5: Use Follow-up Questions.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Build on previous responses in the conversation to maintain flow and context.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> (After a recipe) "Can you suggest a vegetarian alternative for that ingredient?"</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 5: Start New Chats for Related Tasks.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Avoid breaking the flow; keep related queries in the same conversation thread.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> (After a recipe) Start a brand new chat to ask for a vegetarian alternative.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 6: Iterate and Refine.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Your first prompt might not yield the perfect result. Refine and iterate based on its response.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "That was good, but can you make the tone more formal and add a call to action?"</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 6: Give Up After One Try.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Don't expect perfection on the first attempt; refining your prompts is key to better results.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "This isn't what I wanted." (And abandon the conversation)</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 7: Experiment with Tone and Style.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Ask for different writing styles (e.g., informal, academic, poetic, humorous).</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Rewrite this paragraph in the style of a Shakespearean sonnet."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 7: Stick to Generic Outputs.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Don't settle for bland text; push it to match your desired voice or creative style.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Accepting generic text when a specific tone would be more engaging.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 8: Break Down Complex Tasks.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">For large or multi-step projects, tackle one step at a time.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "First, brainstorm 5 ideas for a new mobile app. Second, for the best idea, outline its key features."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 8: Overwhelm with Multi-part Requests.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Avoid asking too many unrelated things in a single, long prompt, which can confuse the AI.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Write a story, then summarize an article, and also give me a recipe for cake."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 9: Use Negative Constraints.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Tell it what to avoid, not just what to include (e.g., "but do not mention battery life").</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Write a product description for a smart watch, but do not mention battery life."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 9: Focus Only on Inclusions.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Don't forget to specify what you *don't* want; this can help narrow down results and prevent unwanted elements.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Just asking for a product description without any exclusions, leading to generic content.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 10: Verify Factual Information.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Always cross-check critical information, as it can sometimes "hallucinate" facts or provide outdated data.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> After getting facts from ChatGPT, quickly verify them with a trusted search engine or source.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 10: Blindly Trust All Outputs.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Don't assume everything it generates is 100% accurate, especially for sensitive or time-critical information.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Using a generated medical diagnosis or legal advice without consulting a professional.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 11: Ask for Explanations.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">If you don't understand something, ask for clarification or simpler terms.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Explain the concept of quantum entanglement in simple terms."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 11: Accept Unclear Responses.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Don't move on if you don't fully grasp its answer; ask it to simplify or elaborate.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Reading a confusing explanation and not asking for a breakdown.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 12: Be Mindful of Data Privacy.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Avoid sharing sensitive personal, confidential, or proprietary information.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Can you help me draft a general email template for client feedback?"</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 12: Input Private or Confidential Data.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Never type in information you wouldn't want publicly known or stored.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "My credit card number is XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX, please summarize my bank statement."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 13: Utilize it for Brainstorming.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">It's an excellent tool for generating a wide range of ideas quickly.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Brainstorm 10 unique gift ideas for someone who loves hiking."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 13: Expect Creative Breakthroughs Without Guidance.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">While creative, it needs direction. Don't expect it to invent something entirely novel from scratch without cues.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Invent a new hit product."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 14: Proofread and Edit.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Always review and refine the generated text for tone, accuracy, and coherence before using it.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> Read through the generated essay and make sure it aligns with your voice and facts.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 14: Copy-Paste Directly.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">The output might contain subtle errors, awkward phrasing, or generic content that needs your human touch.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Pasting a generated email directly without checking for your personal touch or specific details.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 15: Set a Clear Goal.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Know what you want to achieve or learn before you begin typing your prompt.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "I need to write a compelling cover letter for a marketing job."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 15: Prompt Without a Purpose.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Vague goals lead to vague, unhelpful responses; clarify your objective first.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Just tell me something interesting."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 16: Ask for Examples.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">If you want to understand a concept, request illustrative examples to solidify comprehension.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Explain 'supply and demand' and provide a real-world example."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 16: Expect Full Comprehension Without Examples.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Abstract explanations might not suffice; examples often clarify complex ideas more effectively.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Only asking for a definition of a complex term without asking for an example.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 17: Specify the Target Audience.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Tailor the response to who will be reading it (e.g., "explain to a 10-year-old," "for industry professionals").</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Explain blockchain to a 10-year-old."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 17: Forget to Define Your Audience.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">A message for an expert differs significantly from one for a beginner; audience greatly impacts tone and complexity.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Explain blockchain." (Leaving it up to the AI to guess the complexity level.)</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 18: Request Different Perspectives.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Ask it to present information from various viewpoints or arguments for a balanced understanding.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Present the pros and cons of remote work."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 18: Limit it to a Single Viewpoint.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Explore diverse angles for a more comprehensive understanding of a topic.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Only asking "What are the benefits of remote work?" without exploring the drawbacks.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 19: Give Examples of Desired Output.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">If you have a specific style or format in mind, provide a small example in your prompt.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Write a headline like this: 'How to [Verb] Your [Noun] for [Benefit]'. My topic is baking."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 19: Assume it Infers Your Style.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">If you have a very particular writing style, provide an example rather than hoping it understands.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Asking for "creative slogans" without showing what 'creative' means to you.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 20: Correct it Gently.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">If it makes a mistake, point it out politely so it can learn and improve its next response.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Actually, in that last response, the date was incorrect. It should be 2024, not 2023."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 20: Be Rude or Demanding.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">While it's an AI, maintaining a polite and clear tone helps foster better interaction and results.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "You're wrong! Fix this now!"</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 21: Use it as a Learning Tool.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Ask for explanations, summaries, analogies, or simplified versions of complex topics.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Explain the basics of quantum computing for someone with no technical background."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 21: Only Use it for Content Generation.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Its educational capabilities are immense; don't overlook its potential as a personal tutor.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Only asking it to write articles, never asking it to teach you something new.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 22: Test and Validate Generated Code.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Always run and debug any code provided by ChatGPT before using it in a production environment.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Write a Python script to sort a list of numbers. I will test it afterwards to ensure it works correctly."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 22: Deploy Code Without Review.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Don't assume generated code is flawless, secure, or optimized; it always requires human review and testing.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Copying a generated code snippet directly into a live application without testing it.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 23: Break Out of Stuck Conversations.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">If it's repeating itself or giving unhelpful responses, try rephrasing, giving new constraints, or starting fresh.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Let's try that again from a different angle. Instead of X, focus on Y."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 23: Force a Stalled Conversation.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Recognize when a new approach or a fresh chat is needed if the current thread isn't yielding results.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Continuously asking the same question in a loop when the AI isn't understanding.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 24: Ask for Step-by-Step Instructions.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">For procedures, recipes, or complex processes, request a clear, ordered list of steps.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Provide step-by-step instructions for performing CPR."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 24: Expect Intuitive Procedural Knowledge.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Always prompt for explicit, numbered steps for clarity when you need to follow a process.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Asking "How do I make a cake?" and expecting perfectly formatted instructions without asking for steps.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 25: Use Keywords Strategically.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Include important terms or concepts that guide its understanding and focus on relevant information.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Explain the 'water cycle' focusing on 'evaporation,' 'condensation,' and 'precipitation'."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 25: Overly Rely on Natural Language Alone.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">While it understands natural language, specific keywords can help it focus on relevant concepts and improve accuracy.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Tell me about climate." (Instead of "Explain climate change focusing on causes and effects.")</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 26: Ask for Pros and Cons.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Request balanced arguments or a list of advantages and disadvantages for a given topic.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "What are the pros and cons of adopting a minimalist lifestyle?"</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 26: Expect Unbiased Output Without Prompting.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">It often tries to be neutral, but explicitly asking for balanced views ensures a comprehensive perspective.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Tell me why minimalism is good." (Leading to a one-sided response.)</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 27: Clarify Ambiguous Terms.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">If your prompt uses terms with multiple meanings, briefly define what you intend them to mean.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Explain 'cloud computing' (referring to internet-based services, not weather)."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 27: Use Vague Terminology Without Clarification.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">This can lead to misinterpretations or responses that aren't what you were looking for.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Explain 'cloud'."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 28: Use Markdown for Structure (in prompts).</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Specify if you want headings, bullet points, numbered lists, or code blocks in your output.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Summarize this article using markdown headings for each section: [Article Text]"</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 28: Expect Structured Output Without Requesting It.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">If you want nicely formatted text, ask for it; otherwise, you might get a plain paragraph.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> "Give me reasons for exercise." (Expecting a bulleted list without asking for one.)</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 29: Proofread Your Own Prompt.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Errors, typos, or unclear phrasing in your prompt can lead to errors or misunderstandings in the output.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> Quickly read over your prompt before hitting send to catch any mistakes.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 29: Submit Prompts Carelessly.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">A quick review can prevent significant misunderstandings and improve the quality of the response.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Typing a long, complex prompt rapidly without checking for clarity or grammar.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>


                <div class="do-dont-item do">

                    <strong>DO 30: Have Fun and Explore.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">Experiment with its capabilities for creative, playful, and even whimsical uses.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Good:</strong> "Write a short story about a grumpy wizard who loves to bake."</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                <div class="do-dont-item dont">

                    <strong>DON'T 30: Limit Your Imagination.</strong>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">The more you explore its diverse potential, the more valuable a tool it becomes.</p>

                    <div class="example">

                        <p><strong>Bad:</strong> Only using ChatGPT for work-related tasks and never trying its creative functions.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>
